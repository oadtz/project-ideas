### 2.2 Etsy's AI Content Policy

Etsy updated its policy in 2024:
- **AI-generated products are ALLOWED** but must be **disclosed**
- Sellers must select "AI-generated" in listing attributes
- Etsy reserves the right to remove low-quality AI content
- Focus on "handmade" and "unique" â€” mass-produced AI slop risks being flagged
- **Key risk:** Etsy has been deindexing shops suspected of mass AI content without human curation
- Reviews mentioning "AI generated" or "looks automated" can tank a shop's reputation

**Our assessment:** Etsy allows AI but penalizes pure AI slop. The strategy must include human curation, unique branding, and genuine quality differentiation. "AI creates + human curates" is the only viable path.

### 2.4 Quality: AI-Generated vs Human-Designed

**Honest assessment:**

- **Canva social media templates:** AI can produce 6/10 quality. Most look generic. Human designers create more cohesive, branded sets.
- **Google Sheets templates:** AI can produce 8/10 quality. Logic/formulas are AI-friendly. This is our strongest category.
- **Notion templates:** AI can produce 7/10 quality for structure. Visual design limited by Notion's own constraints.

### 4.2 Multi-Marketplace Listing Automation

**Can we automate listing across 4 marketplaces?**

- **Etsy API:** Exists, allows programmatic listings. Doable.
- **Gumroad:** No official bulk listing API. Manual or custom scraping.
- **Creative Market:** No public API. Manual uploads + application process.
- **Shopee:** API exists but for physical products. Digital products are an edge case.

**Realistic automation level:** 
- Etsy: 70% automated (API for listing, manual for mockup uploads)
- Gumroad: 30% automated (manual uploads with pre-written content)
- Creative Market: 10% automated (entirely manual)
- Shopee: Not recommended

**There is no tool that automatically cross-lists digital products across all 4 marketplaces.** Tools like Marmalead, eRank, Sale Samurai are **research tools**, not listing tools.

### 4.3 Quality Control at Scale

The fundamental tradeoff: **quantity kills quality kills revenue.**

- At 500+ products, maintaining consistent quality is extremely difficult
- Each low-quality product hurts shop reputation (reviews, algorithm ranking)
- Etsy's algorithm punishes shops with high browse-to-no-purchase ratios
- Better to have 100 excellent products than 500 mediocre ones
